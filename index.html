About the course

This course is designed for complete beginners in web development. It starts from the very
beginning and covers HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, React.js, Node.js and Express. Along
the way, you will also learn Wordpress, Shopify, Bash Script, GitHub, NPM, FTP Clients, SQL,
Photoshop and other bonus classes.

Where can I take it?

Depends on your choice. If you are interested to take it in class, we are located in Silver Spring, MD. As we are not yet sure about the COVID-19 situation, upcoming classes will only be given online.

What is the difference between the online and the in class course?

Only the medium of teaching is different. Everything else is the same.

How are the online classes given?

The online classes are given using Zoom. Zoom is currently the industry leader software in recreating a digital conference environment. It also provides a way to remotely access computers. This lets us help you on your own computer.

Evangadi Networks

Introduction

Rather than create a situation where one leader mentors an entire group, we prefer to create
more meaningful, one-on-one relationships that leave the opportunity open for greater depth
and impact..

What is it?

It is a network formed by people who believe in the power of collaboration with a common goal of nurturing one another to grow together.

How is it different?

While most networks focus exclusively on growing the largest network possible, Evangadi Networks strongly believes in the power of cultivating deeper, more meaningful connections between members.

Who can join?

Anyone who believes in the power of collaboration can join the network; the only requirement is the understanding that collaboration is a two-way transaction.

What is the Vision?

In the short term, we want to stay focused on helping everyone in the network improve their skills and knowledge in the digital world.

The on-boarding steps

Since we are aiming to create a deeper relationship among members, it is critical to identify
and match people with similar interests and goals.

What happens here?

When someone decides to join, they will be asked a series of questions to help us determine the best way to place that person within the system.

Who does the matching?

Most placement is handled automatically by the algorithm on our website. In some unique cases where the algorithm cannot find a perfect fit, leaders of the groups will take a look and help the newcomer find a perfect mentor that matches their needs.

How would I find about my groups and mentor?

You can access all of the groups and mentors that you are associated with when you login to your account on evangadi.com. online.

Can I choose my own mentor?

Yes you can. All of the available mentors will be listed to you to choose from.

Interest Groups

These are interest specific communities that are roughly organized based on the
knowledge level of the members..

Where can I find and join groups?

Groups that are aligned with your interests will be suggested to you as you go through the on-boarding process.

Can I join multiple groups?

Yes you can. Groups are open for everyone interested. The only thing is, you can't become a mentor in a Group that you do not have enough knowledge about the field.

Are there any non tech groups?

Yes there are. But we are currently focused on the tech groups. The others might not get as much attention and support from Evangadi's technical support team.

Can I create my own group?

Yes you can. But you are required to be an expert in the field of interest group you are planning to start.

The mentor-ship Unit

A deeper community can be far more impactful than a wide one. The mentorship Units are
where we focus a lot to create that meaningful relationship among members..

Who gets to be a mentor?

Everyone can be a mentor. A 3rd grader might have much to offer to a younger student just entering the 2nd grade.

How can I find the right mentor and join his/her Unit?

In addition to the recommendations the system makes based on the information you provide when joining the Network, all open Units will be listed under their respective groups along with the mentor's information and a description given by the mentor.

What are my responsibilities if I become a mentor?

Mentoring is completely voluntary and how much time you dedicate is 100% decided by you. When you become a mentor, you are asked to create a group chat for the members of your unit and answer their questions as much you can. We encourage lower members to help each other when possible, but the mentor should be there to offer assistance as much as possible.

Partners of Evangadi

These are educational institutions, organizations, and individuals who are collaborating with
Evangadi to further assist our members with courses that can help members quickly
advance to a higher skill level.

Who are the partners?

These are educational institutions, organizations, and individuals who are collaborating with Evangadi to further assist our members with courses that can help members quickly advance to a higher skill level.

How can I become a partner with Evangadi?

To become a partner, you need to have a well-designed course that we believe would be helpful to our members. We will do a thorough evaluation of your organization and offered courses and will accept your partnership request if we believe in the overall quality and value of what you have to offer. Once accepted, your company will be added to our partners list.

Does Evangadi play any role in designing or setting the prices for partner courses?

No! Partners design their own courses, decide their own prices, and run their own classes independently. We do not involve or interfere with how partners do their business. The only thing we do is recommend courses that we believe will benefit our members.
